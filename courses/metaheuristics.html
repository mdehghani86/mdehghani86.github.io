OR6500

Metaheuristics and Applications

Master nature-inspired optimization algorithms including genetic algorithms, simulated annealing, particle swarm optimization, and their applications to complex engineering problems.

Fall
4 Credits
Graduate Level

Course Overview

This course provides comprehensive coverage of metaheuristic optimization algorithms, which are powerful techniques for solving complex optimization problems where traditional methods fall short. Students will learn both the theoretical foundations and practical implementations of these nature-inspired algorithms.


The curriculum covers evolutionary algorithms, swarm intelligence, trajectory-based methods, and hybrid approaches. Students will apply these techniques to real-world problems in scheduling, routing, resource allocation, and engineering design optimization.

Learning Objectives

1
Understand the theoretical foundations of metaheuristic optimization
2
Implement genetic algorithms, PSO, simulated annealing, and other methods
3
Analyze algorithm performance and convergence behavior
4
Design hybrid metaheuristics for specific problem domains
5
Apply metaheuristics to real-world engineering optimization problems
