Course Overview

This advanced course provides a rigorous introduction to reinforcement learning, a paradigm of machine learning where agents learn to make optimal decisions through interaction with their environment. The course covers foundational concepts of Markov Decision Processes, dynamic programming, and temporal difference learning.


Students will progress from classical RL algorithms to modern deep reinforcement learning techniques, including Deep Q-Networks (DQN), Policy Gradient methods, and Actor-Critic architectures. The curriculum emphasizes engineering applications in robotics, operations research, and autonomous systems.

Learning Objectives

1
Formulate sequential decision problems as Markov Decision Processes
2
Implement value-based and policy-based RL algorithms from scratch
3
Apply deep reinforcement learning to complex continuous control problems
4
Understand exploration-exploitation trade-offs and algorithm convergence
5
Design RL solutions for real-world engineering optimization problems
